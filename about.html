Introduction

What really is Rome?

Rome is a city with a rich historical background and has impacted Europe positvely, from languages to the legal system.



Language

The language the Romans spoke is called "Latin". Latin became the foundation of the "Romance" languages such as Italian, French, Spanish, Portuguese, and Romanian. The Latin alphabet is widely used in Europe and has become the standard writing system for numerous languages



Legal System

The development of the "Roman Law" has had an significant long lasting effect on Europe. The principles of the Roman Law such as the concept of 'Innocent until proven guilty' and the importance of legal precedence formed the groundwork for majority of legal systems in Europe.

History of Rome

Major periods in Roman history

  • 753 BCE - 509 BCE

    Roman Kingdom

    Founded by Romulus and Remus in 753 BC

    characterized by the rule of monarchs, including the legendary kings and the establishment of early Roman institutions.

  • 509 BCE - 27 BCE

    Roman Republic

    Established after overthrowing the previous king.

    Expanded its territory through conquest, featuring The Punic Wars against Carthage.

    Key figures such as Julius Caesar, Pompey and Crassus played big roles in Roman politics and formed the "First Triumvirate"in 60 BC.

    Julius Caesar crosses the Rubicon in 49 BC which led to a civil war, resulting in the end of the Republic

  • 27 BCE - 476 CE

    Roman Empire

    Augustus became the first Roman Emperor after defeating Mark Antony in 31 BCE.

    Peace across Rome emerged called the "Pax Romana" meaning Roman Peace.

    Reaches its greatest territorial extend under Emperor Trajan.

    Western Roman Empire started to decline due to economic and military difficulties.

    Romulus Agustulus is dethroned in 476 CE which marked the fall of the Western Roman Empire

  • 330 CE - 1453 CE

    Byzantine Empire (Eastern Roman Empire)

    Continued nearly a thousand years after the collapse of the Western Roman Empire.

    Faced challenges such as invasions and conflicts with the Persian Empire (later known as the Ottoman Turks).

    Ended up falling to the Ottoman Turks in 1453, marking the end of the Byzantine Empire

Present Day Rome

What is Rome like now?

Streets

The streets of Rome is compact and have narrow alleys and preserve their rich history with many factors such as the streets being paved in Cobblestone.

Scenery

Rome is sometimes referred to as the "City of the seven hills" due to the landscape it was built on. These hills provided massive help in the development of early Rome and today, they provide people with breath-taking scenery.

Nature

Due to Rome being Mediterranean, the various plants here thrive in this environment. Rome is famous for their rich historical background, however the natural side of it contributes to the aesthetics and the overall character of the city.

Summary

Rome is a city with incredibly rich history that played a crucial role in its unique charm. The streets of Rome has a mixture of historical glamour and modern vibrancy, giving the feeling of a timeless crossroad where history meets the present
